Si Thep Historical Park is an archaeological site located in Phetchabun Province, Thailand. It preserves the ruins of an ancient town that dates back to the Dvaravati period, roughly between the 6th and 11th centuries. The park includes remnants of city walls, moats, temples, and religious monuments, showcasing early urban planning and Buddhist cultural influence in central Thailand. Visitors can walk among the well-preserved ruins, admire ancient Buddha images and sculptures, and explore the on-site museum that displays artifacts such as pottery, tools, and religious objects. The park provides insight into early Thai civilization, highlighting the artistry, spirituality, and daily life of the people who once lived there. It is both an educational and visually striking destination for history enthusiasts and tourists.
